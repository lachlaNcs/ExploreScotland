Kelvingrove Art Gallery

Located in the West End of Glasgow, the Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum is one of Scotland’s most popular attractions. It houses an impressive collection of artworks and historical exhibits, offering something for everyone, from Salvador Dalí’s "Christ of St John of the Cross" to ancient Egyptian artifacts.

George Square

At the heart of Glasgow, George Square serves as a cultural and historical hub. It is surrounded by Victorian architecture and features statues of renowned figures such as Sir Walter Scott. The square hosts various events, including festivals and markets.

Riverside Museum

The Riverside Museum, located on the banks of the River Clyde, showcases Glasgow’s transport heritage. Its modern architecture, designed by Zaha Hadid, complements its extensive collection of vintage vehicles, including trams, cars, and bicycles.

Glasgow Cathedral

Glasgow Cathedral, also known as St. Mungo's Cathedral, is an iconic medieval building that boasts stunning Gothic architecture. As the oldest cathedral in mainland Scotland, it is a site of significant historical and religious importance.
