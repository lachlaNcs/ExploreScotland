Discover More About Edinburgh's Landmarks

Learn about the rich history and beauty of Edinburgh's iconic sites.

Edinburgh Castle

Perched on Castle Rock, Edinburgh Castle is a historic fortress that has played a pivotal role in Scotland’s history. It offers breathtaking views of the city and houses the Crown Jewels and the Stone of Destiny.

Arthur's Seat

A dormant volcano in Holyrood Park, Arthur’s Seat offers panoramic views of Edinburgh. A popular hiking destination, it is steeped in history and myths, offering a serene escape from the city.

Scotts Monument

Standing proudly in Princes Street Gardens, the Scott Monument is one of the most iconic Edinburgh landmarks, a must-visit for tourists and locals alike. Dedicated to Sir Walter Scott, it is one of the largest monuments to a writer anywhere in the world.

Holyrood Palace

The Palace of Holyroodhouse, the official residence of the British monarch in Scotland, is rich in history and grandeur. Visitors can explore the palace’s state apartments and the ruins of Holyrood Abbey.
